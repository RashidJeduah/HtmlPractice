The West African Country, Ghana.

Nestled amongst the francophone countries of Cote D'Ivoire, Burkina Faso, Togo, and Benin, Ghana is one of the few West African countries with an Anglophone population. Right at the coast of the Gulf of Guinea, Ghana has a population of about 27 million people

Demographics of Ghana

Ghana is a multiethnic, multireligious society, with a about a 100 and more ethnic groups and languages, and predominantly 3 religions. Majority of Ghanaians are christaians, with a larger minority being Muslims, and the smallest minority, Traditionalists. Known for its warm and welcoming people, and a beacon of democracy on the African continent. Some of the languages in Ghana include:

  • Gonja
  • Fante
  • Twi
  • Ga
  • Ewe
  • Dagaare

Interestingly, Ghana has had 4 Presidents in succession called John:

  1. John Rawlings
  2. John Kuffuor
  3. John Mills
  4. John Mahama
